Writing better project case studies.

A case study should show how you think, not just what the final screen looked like.

Good case studies make your work easier to trust. They explain the reason behind the design and show that you can move from problem to solution.

Describe the challenge

Start with what was unclear, broken, missing, or difficult for the user. This gives your decisions meaning.

Explain your process

Mention research, wireframes, layout choices, responsive decisions, content structure, and important tradeoffs. Keep it simple and honest.

Share the result

Even if the project is personal, explain what improved: clarity, usability, speed, visual quality, or maintainability.
